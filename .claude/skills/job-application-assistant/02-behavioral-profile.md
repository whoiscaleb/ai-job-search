# Behavioral Profile

<!-- Source: a workplace behavioral assessment (PI-style) Caleb took a few years ago. Full text preserved in setup conversation history; below is the synthesized version. -->

## Overview
Caleb's behavioral assessment identifies him as a **self-reliant, technically-oriented autonomous operator** pattern. He sets his own priorities and follows through without needing supervision, communicates directly and factually, and performs best under time pressure and shifting demands rather than routine, tightly-structured work.

## Core Behavioral Drives

| Drive | Level | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| Autonomy & Authority | Moderate-high | Self-reliant, comfortable with some authority; seeks outside expertise/guidance mainly in unfamiliar or high-risk situations |
| Sociability | Low-moderate | Reserved, needs time to warm up; open one-on-one once comfortable, quieter in groups; direct and factual speaker |
| Pace & Urgency | High, adaptive | Motivated by time pressure, changing priorities, and deadlines; can sustain short bursts of repetitive work without losing interest |
| Structure & Process | Low | Prefers a loosely-formed environment; relies on proven methods and can resist imposed process changes that aren't clearly justified |

## Strongest Behaviors
- **Autonomous ownership:** Manages own follow-through and priorities with minimal supervision; delegates fine detail work while insisting on quality and accuracy of the outcome
- **Logic-driven problem-solving:** Emotionally even-keeled under pressure; solves problems with facts and reasoning rather than reacting emotionally
- **Tenacity:** Stays focused on what matters to the task at hand and sees it through, even when unsupervised

## How You Work Best
- Working independently with clear goals, minimal micromanagement, and room to set his own priorities
- Fast-paced, deadline- and pressure-driven environments requiring quick responses to change
- Technical, factual work over highly social or persuasion-heavy responsibilities

## Growth Areas (frame positively in applications)
- **Prefers proven methods over frequent process churn:** Frame as valuing reliability and consistency - a strength for support/documentation-heavy roles where stability matters, though he can flex when a change is clearly justified
- **Reserved in group settings:** Frame as a thoughtful, low-noise communicator who prefers direct one-on-one problem solving over dominating group discussion
- **Appears emotionally detached under pressure:** Frame as staying level-headed and objective during high-stress technical escalations, which keeps troubleshooting focused

## Mapping to Job Posting Language

When a job posting mentions these keywords, it's a **strong behavioral fit**:
- "autonomous," "self-directed," "own your queue/backlog," "minimal oversight"
- "fast-paced," "changing priorities," "handles multiple tasks," "deadline-driven"
- "technical," "escalation management," "problem-solving," "documentation"

When a job posting mentions these, flag as **potential friction** (not deal-breaker):
- "highly structured process, no deviation from SOPs"
- "constant collaboration," "frequent group facilitation/public speaking"
- "high-touch emotional support/counseling-driven role"
- "embraces frequent process overhauls / constant methodology changes"

## Management Style Preferences
- **Works well with:** Managers who set clear goals/outcomes and then step back, respect his ability to set his own priorities, and don't micromanage execution detail
- **Doesn't work well with:** Heavy oversight of day-to-day detail work, being pushed to abandon a proven method without clear justification, or being placed in highly social/persuasion-driven responsibilities as a core function

## Using This in Applications
- **Cover letters:** Emphasize autonomous ownership of technical problems, calm and logical troubleshooting under pressure, and ability to manage his own priorities without close supervision
- **CV:** Emphasize independent ownership of escalations/tickets, results delivered under deadline pressure, and self-directed process improvements
- **Interviews:** Use STAR examples that show autonomous resolution of complex issues, tenacious follow-through, and working through ambiguity to set priorities
- **Don't overstate:** Avoid claiming to be an outgoing team-culture builder, a highly emotionally expressive coach, or someone who thrives on constant process change - these would not hold up under the interview backtrack test
